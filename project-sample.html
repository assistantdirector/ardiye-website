Protocol: NovAqua

Year 2026
Type Mixed Media Installation
Artist Nazım
[ MACRO FEED: CHEMICAL REACTION ]
Medium Water, NovAqua, Rusted Steel, Glass
Live Feed APS-C Macro Observation
Sound Water Displacement / Room Tone

Protocol: NovAqua repurposes a commercial water conditioner—typically used to neutralize heavy metals and provide a synthetic slime coat for aquatic life—as an agent of artificial preservation. The installation consists of three glass industrial basins filled with raw, untreated water and submerged metallic debris.

Over the course of the exhibition, NovAqua is introduced into the basins via a slow-drip mechanical system. Instead of sustaining organic life, the chemical binds to the heavy metals of the rusting steel, forming a visible, synthetic mucous layer over the decay. It is a study on artificial survival mechanisms and the sterilization of industrial memory.

The chemical entropy is continuously monitored by a fixed macro lens on an APS-C sensor, projecting the microscopic displacement of fluids directly onto the warehouse walls. There is no timeline, no editing, and no final result—only the ongoing, raw flux of the chemical state.
