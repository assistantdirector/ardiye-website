Ambient Architecture

Year 2025
Type A/V Installation
Artist Nazım
[ RAW FOOTAGE / AUDIO WAVEFORM ]
Cinematography Nazım
Soundscapes Ardiye Team
Format Sony FX30 / Unmastered
Location Copenhagen / Luxembourg

Ambient Architecture is a study of how low-frequency sound and sustained visual observation shape our perception of empty, decaying spaces. Moving away from the hyper-speed of modern digital montage, the installation forces the viewer to confront the raw physical scale of abandoned industrial structures.

The visual foundation relies on extended, static takes shot on APS-C sensors. The footage deliberately bypasses any polished color grading processes in software like DaVinci Resolve, preserving the native noise and flat texture of the original environment.

Acoustically, the space is defined by heavy, post-rock inspired slow guitar loops and thick ambient drones. As the sound waves bounce off the gallery walls, they mimic the spatial memory of the architectural structures seen on screen. It is a documentation of process and presence, left entirely in a state of raw flux.
